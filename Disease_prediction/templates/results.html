  • -> Use resources like counseling, nicotine replacement therapy, and support groups to help you quit smoking.

  • -> If you drink alcohol, do so in moderation. For adults, this means up to one drink per day for women and up to two drinks per day for men.

  • -> Avoid unhealthy coping mechanisms such as overeating, smoking, or drinking excessively.

  • -> Have regular check-ups with your healthcare provider to monitor your heart health and receive personalized advice.

  • -> Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night. Poor sleep can contribute to heart disease risk factors like high blood pressure, obesity, and diabetes.

  • -> Maintain a regular sleep schedule, create a comfortable sleep environment, and avoid caffeine and heavy meals before bedtime.

  • -> Stay hydrated by drinking water throughout the day. Proper hydration supports overall health and can help maintain blood pressure levels.
